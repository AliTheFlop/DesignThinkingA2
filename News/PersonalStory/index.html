Brisbane's Heatwaves Are Changing My Life

I've lived in Brisbane my whole life, growing up in a Queenslander in Moorooka. Summers once meant river swims and family barbecues—now they feel endless and suffocating. In March 2023, a night of 28°C heat triggered a severe asthma attack in my toddler. Floods worse than 2011 followed; a neighbour in Rocklea had to abandon their home after repeated damage and denied insurance. Even nature seems off—silent frogs, falling jacarandas, and heat-struck bats in winter. Record highs are now routine. My sister lost everything in the Lismore floods and moved back. We've made small changes—solar panels, shorter showers—but it feels like bailing out a sinking boat with a teaspoon. The heat wears you down emotionally. Brisbane still has beauty, but the climate crisis has made our seasons unreliable and our futures uncertain. I miss the Brisbane I knew and worry my son will grow up thinking this is normal.

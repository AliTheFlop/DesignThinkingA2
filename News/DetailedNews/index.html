Brisbane's Green Future

Major Reforestation Projects Take Root Across the City

Brisbane is going green with a major reforestation campaign by the City Council, targeting degraded and underused land across the city. Native trees are being planted along the Brisbane River, in the western suburbs, and in bushland reserves to boost biodiversity, cool urban areas, and restore koala habitats. ‚ÄúThis is about reconnecting ecosystems,‚Äù said Councillor Jane Thompson. The project involves ecologists, Traditional Custodians, and community groups, with volunteers, schools, and local businesses invited to take part. With over 80,000 trees planted since 2023, the program is already showing positive signs like rising bird numbers and healthier soil. It also supports Brisbane's goal of being carbon neutral by 2050. Residents can learn more or join in via the Green Brisbane portal.
